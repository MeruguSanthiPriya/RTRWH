Rainwater Harvesting – Measurement & Purification

Quality of Rainwater
  • Salts: Very low naturally; may increase if stored in concrete or mixed with soil. High salts cause scaling; not suitable for drinking without treatment.
  • pH: Pure rain pH ≈ 5.5–6.5 (slightly acidic). Ideal drinking pH: 6.5–8.5.
  • Impurities: Dust, leaves, bird droppings; acid rain may add nitrates/sulfates.
Is Rainwater Useful?
  • Drinking: Yes, after advanced filtration + pH adjustment.
  • Daily Use (wells, flushing, washing, irrigation): Yes, after simple filtration.

Purification Measures

A. Drinking Water

  • First Flush Diverter → removes initial dirty water
  • Sand + Charcoal Filter → removes dust, color, odor
  • UV/RO or Ceramic Filter → removes microbes; RO if salts/metals
  • pH Correction → lime or mineral cartridge to balance pH
Required pH: 6.5–8.5

B. Daily Usage (Wells/Household/Irrigation)

  • Mesh Filters / Gutter Screens → block leaves and debris
  • Sand/Gravel Filter → remove suspended particles
  • Settling Tank → allow heavy particles to settle
pH range tolerated: 6.0–8.5

Detailed Process

1) Sedimentation

Purpose: Remove coarse suspended solids by gravity before filters. Target detention: 30–60 min for households; longer for larger systems.

  • Calm inlet; sediment/grit trap; first-flush diverter (first 1–2 mm rainfall)
  • Outlet from below surface, above sludge
  • Maintenance: empty first-flush after events; desludge every 1–6 months

2) Filtration

  • Coarse: Gutter screens; 10–50 µm bag/cartridge pre-filter
  • Media: Rapid/multimedia (ESD 0.2–0.5 mm), or slow sand
  • Activated Carbon: Taste/odor, organics
  • Fine/Membrane: 5→1 µm cartridge; UF (~0.01 µm) or RO (salts/metals)

3) Disinfection

  • UV: ~40 mJ/cm², needs clear water; no residual
  • Chlorination: Maintain 0.2–0.5 mg/L free chlorine

4) Final Polishing / pH Adjustment

  • Raise pH if < 6.5 using lime/soda ash or calcite cartridge
  • Remineralize RO permeate with calcite or blending

Quick Comparison

Drinking
First-flush + settling + media + carbon + UF/RO + UV; pH 6.5–8.5
Daily/Wells
First-flush + settling + mesh + sand/gravel; optional chlorination
